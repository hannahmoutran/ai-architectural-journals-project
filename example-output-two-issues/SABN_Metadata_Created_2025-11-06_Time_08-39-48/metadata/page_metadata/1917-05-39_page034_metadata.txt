Want Subinspector of Construction.
The United States Civil Service Commission announces an open competitive examination for subinspector of construction for men only. A considerable number of vacancies at various navy yards and other naval establishments of the United States, at entrance salaries ranging from $3.28 to $5.52 per diem, will be filled from this examination.
In filling vacancies certification will be made of the highest eligibles residing in the vicinity of the place at which the appointee is to be employed, except that upon the request of the Navy Department certification will be made of the highest eligibles on the register for the entire country who have expressed willingness to accept appointment where the vacancy exists.
Competitors will not be required to report for examination at any place, but will be rated on the following subjects, which will have the relative weights indicated, on a scale of 100: (1) Education, 40; (2) experience, 60.
Competitors who fail to attain a rating of at least 70 per cent under the subject of experience will not be considered for appointment.
In rating the subject of experience the degree of difficulty and importance of the work performed, the length of experience, and the rating which an applicant has had as an inspector or workman will be considered.
Statements as to education, training, and experience are accepted subject to verification.
Applicants must have reached their twentieth birthday on the date of filing application.
Until further notice, owing to the lack of eligibles, the number of appointments to be made, and the urgent needs of the service, applications will be received at any time, and the papers will be rated immediately upon their receipt in order that appointments may be made with the least possible delay.
Class No. 1, $3.28 to $4.24 per Diem—
Applicants must have had the education, training, and experience specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduate of a high school or preparatory school and two years' actual experience in building inspection or construction.
(b) Two years in a technical school and three years' experience in building inspection or construction; or three years in a technical and two years of such experience.
(c) Four years' actual experience in building inspection or construction.
Class No. 2, $4.48 to $5.52 per Diem—
Applicants must have had the education, training, and experience specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduate of a college of recognized standing and two years' actual experience in building inspection or construction.
(b) Graduate of a high school or preparatory school, two years in a technical school, and four years' experience in building inspection or construction.
(c) Six years' actual experience in building inspection or construction.
Applicants should be men of good practical experience in one of the building trades. They should have a fair common school education, and be capable of reading drawings, making measurements of work, and calculating quantities of materials in place.
This examination is open to all male citizens of the United States who meet the requirements.
Applicants should at once apply for Form 1312, stating the title of the examination desired, to the Civil Service Commission, Washington, D.
