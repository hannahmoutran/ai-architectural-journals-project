SOUTHERN ARCHITECT AND BUILDING NEWS

Want Architectural Draftsman.

The United States Civil Service Commission announces an open competitive examination for architectural draftsman, for men only. From the register of eligibles resulting from this examination certification will be made to fill a considerable number of vacancies in this position in the Bureau of Yards and Docks, Navy Department, Washington, D. C., and at navy yards and other naval establishments of the United States, at salaries ranging from $3.04 to $6 per diem.

In filling vacancies in navy yards and other naval establishments certification will be made of the highest eligibles residing in the vicinity of the place at which the appointee is to be employed, except that upon the request of the Navy Department certification will be made of the highest eligibles on the register for the entire country who have expressed willingness to accept appointment where the vacancy exists.

The duties of these positions will be architectural drafting and the preparation of working drawings, specifications, and details.

Competitors will not be required to report for examination at any place, but will be rated on the following subjects, which will have the relative weights indicated, on a scale of 100: (1) Education and preliminary training, 40; (2) experience, 60.

Competitors who fail to attain a rating of at least 70 per cent under the subject of experience will not be considered for appointment.

In rating the subject of experience, the degree of difficulty and importance of the work performed, the length of experience, and the rating which an applicant has had as a draftsman or workman will be considered.

Statements as to education, training, and experience are accepted subject to verification.

Applicants must not have reached their fiftieth birthday on the date of filing application.

Until further notice, owing to the lack of eligibles, the number of appointments to be made, and the urgent needs of the service, applications will be received at any time, and the papers will be rated immediately upon their receipt in order that appointments may be made with the least possible delay.

The register of eligibles will be divided into four grades, the compensation and minimum requirements for each grade being as follows:
Grade 1, $3.04 to $3.84 per diem—

Applicants must have the education, training, and experience as specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduation from a four years’ high school or four full years of study in a school offering a course equivalent to that of a high school, and two years of architectural drafting.
(b) Two years of technical school training and one year of architectural drafting.
(c) Three years of architectural drafting. Grade 2, $4 to $5.04 per diem—

Applicants must have the education, training, and experience as specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduation in an architectural course from a college of recognized standing and two years of architectural drafting.
(b) Two years of technical school training and three years of architectural drafting.
(c) Five years of architectural drafting. Grade 3, $5.28 to $6 per diem—

Applicants must have the education, training, and experience as specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduation in an architectural course from a college of recognized standing and four years of architectural drafting.
(b) Two years of technical school training and six years of architectural drafting.
(c) Seven years of architectural drafting. Grade 4, $6.24 to $8 per diem—

Applicants must have the education, training, and experience as specified in one of the following groups:
(a) Graduation in an architectural course from a college of recognized standing and six years of architectural drafting and designing.
(b) Three years of technical school training and eight years of architectural drafting and designing.
(c) Twelve years of architectural experience, of which not less than three shall have been in design.

Applicants will be admitted to this examination regardless of their residence and domicile, but those desiring appointment to the apportioned service in Washington, D. C., must have been actually domiciled in the State or Territory in which they reside for at least one year previous to the date of making oath to the application, and must have the county officer’s certificate in the application form executed.

This examination is open to all male citizens of the United States who meet the requirements.
