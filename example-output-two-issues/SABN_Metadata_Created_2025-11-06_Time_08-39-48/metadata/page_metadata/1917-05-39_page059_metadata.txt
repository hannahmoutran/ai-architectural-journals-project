SOUTHERN ARCHITECT AND BUILDING NEWS

"WRIALCO"
A PATENTED CONCRETE INSERT
The Most Practical and Reasonable Priced Hanger Yet Devised for Automatic Sprinkler, Shafting and General Piping Work in Connection with Concrete Building Construction

Fig. 2.

Fig. 1.

Figure 1 shows the hanger as embedded in concrete.
Figure 2 shows the perspective view of hanger, also showing nut to which the rod or bolt is attached and the adjustment feature. The rod or bolt can be made rigid at any point of adjustment desired.

The insert is fastened to form or false work by wire nails on either side bent over at the top or by bolting direct to the nut which is placed within the adjustment chamber, removing the bolt when taking down forms.

It's a simple operation and the hanger proposition is solved.
Thousands in use.
Owners, Architects and Construction Engineers specify them.
Made in four sizes, for 7/16, ½, ⅝ and ¾ nuts.
Descriptive matter and prices upon application.

WRIGHT & ALEXANDER COMPANY
ROCHESTER, NEW YORK
