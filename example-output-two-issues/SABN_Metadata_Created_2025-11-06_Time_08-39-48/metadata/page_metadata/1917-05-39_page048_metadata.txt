Houston, Tex.—Trinity Episcopal Church, Rev. Clinton S. Quin, Rector, has plans by Wm. Ward Watkin, of Cram & Ferguson, Houston, for church and parish-house; main church auditorium 45x120 ft.; Gothic style; seating capacity 600; parish-house with auditorium to seat 400; 20 classrooms, kitchen, lavatory, etc.; cost $75,000.

Orange, Tex.—Lutcher Memorial Presbyterian Church will erect chapel to seat 100. Address The Pastor.

Quanah, Tex.—Methodist Church will erect building; stone construction; exterior finished in white pebble dash; 102x73 ft. Address The Pastor.

Yoakum, Tex.—First Methodist Church receives bids until April 30 to erect $30,000 brick church building; plans and specifications from L. L. Stephenson, Yoakum, and Finger & Bailey, Archt's, Houston, Tex.

Richmond, Va.—Riverview Baptist Church, colored, will remodel building; cost about $10,000. Address The Pastor.

Cotton Plant, Ark.—Baptist Church plans to erect parsonage. Address The Pastor.

Earle, Ark.—Baptist Church will erect $10,000 brick building; construction under supervision Rev. A. P. Schofield, Pastor.

Schools and Colleges.

Alcorn, Miss.—Alcorn Agricultural & Mechanical College, James McLeod, secretary-treasurer & executive committee, Fayette, Miss., will rebuild structures recently damaged by fire.

Anderson, S. C.—Architect Jas. J. Baldwin, Anderson, will prepare plans for $80,000 high school building recently noted.

Ellenton, Fla.—Manatee Board of Public Instruction, T. M. McDuffie, chairman, Bradentown, Fla., rejected bids to erect new school building here; board adopted plans prepared by Architect A. C. Price, Bradentown, and will ask for new bids. Plans, etc., may be obtained from B. D. Gullett, Superintendent of Public Instruction, Bradentown.

Jackson, Ga.—Election will be held May 15 upon issuance $22,000 of municipal school building bonds. The Mayor.

Millen, Ga.—Architect G. Lloyd Preacher, Augusta, Ga., is preparing plans for $150,000 high school building.

Sandersville, Ga.—Architect J. F. Leitner, Atlanta, Ga., and Wilmington, N. C., will prepare plans for $20,000 school building previously noted; high school equipment and departments.

Trenton, S. C.—High school building will be erected at cost of $150,000. Plans are being prepared by Architect G. Lloyd Preacher, Augusta, Ga.

Woodrow, Fla.—Special Tax School District No. 12 will erect school buildings at Woodrow and New Prospect Landing. Lee County Board of Public Instruction, Fort Myers.

Attalla, Ala.—City voted $11,000 bonds to improve streets and schools. Address The Mayor.

Rich Mountain, Ark.—School Board will erect building to replace lately-burned structure.

Arcadia, Fla.—De Soto County Board of Public Instruction sold $35,000 bond issue and will soon call for bids to erect grammar school; 10 rooms; semi-fireproof; brick walls; metal lath ceilings; composition roof; steam heat; electric lights; A. C. Price, Archt.; J. Rice Scott, Engr. Associated, both of Arcadia.

Laurel Hill, Fla.—Laurel Hill Special Tax School Dist. has plans by S. J. Welch, Pensacola, Fla., and opens bids May 7 to erect school; 93x76 ft.; 2 stories; brick; asphalt shingle roof; pine floors; steam heat; independent electric light plant; cost $12,000. Address W. C. Pryor, Crestview, Fla.

Groveland, Fla.—Groveland School Dist. voted $7000 bonds to erect school building. Address Dist. School Trustees.

Lakeland, Fla.—Polk County Board of Public Instruction opens bids May 8 to erect addition to school building; 76x68 ft.; frame; cypress shingle roof; cost $3000; Bryan & Potete, Archts., Lakeland.

Ocala, Fla.—J. V. Burke, Secy. Board of Commrs. of State Institutions, Tallahassee, receives bids until May 15 to erect Industrial School for Girls; plans and specifications at office W. B. Taley and H. F. Saxelbye, Archts., St. James Bldg., Room 338, Jacksonville.

Hoffman, N. C.—School Board will erect school building; tapestry brick and limestone trimming; 6 rooms and auditorium; steam heat; cost $10,000; L. L. Hunter, Archt., Charlotte, N. C.

Lexington, N. C.—Lexington School Dist., will not erect school building as lately noted.

Statesville, N. C.—City votes June 19 on $25,000 graded school bonds; R. M. Gray, Supt. City Board of Education.

Woodland, N. C.—School Board did not let contract to erect school but will have work prepared by local architects; Wm. Newton Dibrell, Archt., 46-47 Chamberlain Bldg., Norfolk, Va.

Knoxville, Tenn.—City is considering issuing $800,000 for Greater Knoxville for school, street repairs, sewers, water mains, hospitals and parks. John E. McMillan, Mayor.

Yorkville, Tenn.—Yorkville Special School Dist., F. R. Utley, Secy., will erect building; 60x80 ft.; brick, or in some other flat roof; wood floors; cost $10,000; arch. not selected. Address Mr. Utley.
